I have a new webpage, so it seems worth laying out where things stand in July 2026.

I am currently ranked sixth in the world with 14.10 seconds. Third on the list is 13.99 and fourth is 14.00, which means I am about a tenth of a second from both fourth place and the podium. That is the scale my sport operates on. A tenth or two in either direction and the picture changes completely. I have been working on that gap for twenty years, and at 37 I am closer to it than I have ever been.

I opened my season in Switzerland with three races and the best result from that trip was 14.10s. The Finnish summer since then has been reasonable without being what I was after. I came into it looking for a personal best and I am still looking. The season average is the best of my career so far, though, and I have usually been at my sharpest in August and September, so there is still a fair amount of season left to run and to improve.

I am currently second in the European rankings, which is part of why the absence of a European Championships this year is frustrating. There is no Europeans and no Worlds in 2026. The athlete ranked ahead of me in Europe is also second in the world, and a European final against him would have told me something genuinely useful about where I stand. Instead, every race this summer takes place with nobody paying much attention except me, my coach, and the ranking list. The year either builds something towards the next two years, or not.

I finished fifth in the world in 2024 and seventh in 2025, and I do not receive the government athlete grant. I am not making an accusation there. The pot is finite and the criteria are what they are. But the arithmetic is worth putting on the page. A season costs me somewhere around 20,000 euros. That is costs, not income. Racing chairs, training camps, flights, physiotherapy, competition entries, equipment that wears out and has to be replaced. It does not include actually living, and living has to be paid for as well. Full-time athletics is a job that pays precariously and asks for everything, and it is funded by sponsors, by savings, and if you get lucky, grants and system funding.

The plan from here is the World Championships in Tashkent in June 2027, then Los Angeles in 2028, and that would be the end of it. Unless I stop after this season, which I am putting in writing because pretending it is not a possibility would be dishonest. If the situation stays as it is, I have to call it quits at the end of this year, which feels hilarious if and when I am at my best level ever.

The governance work is the one part of my life that is not being postponed. I chair the athletes' commission at the Finnish Olympic Committee and hold board seats at the NOC and at Finnish Athletics. It is unpaid and it takes up time I could be selling elsewhere, but it is also the closest thing I have to building something for afterwards while still competing.

And if I stopped competing tomorrow, I would almost certainly be better at it. More time, more energy, no conflict of interest, no arguing about athlete funding while quietly worrying about my own. I could push harder on grants, on Paralympic parity, on the structural issues that need pushing. And in doing so I would make the athlete space slightly worse, because there would be one fewer Finn in a world final and one fewer person demonstrating that it can be done. Being most useful to athletes would require me to stop being one. I do not have an answer to that, and I am not convinced there is one.

Last autumn, after the World Championships, I wrote a long reflection on the season, maybe uploading it here at some point too. I finished 7th place, 14.15, and I crossed the line and felt nothing at all. I ended the text by saying that the competitor in me was waking up again and I was going to let it happen. Nine months on, it is awake and was preparing for a Europeans that ended up not happening. Now it should remain awake for the rest of the season.

A tenth of a second, two seasons left in all likelihood, still training, still after the personal best. I do not know whether everything will work out. I do know what it would feel like to stop with the question still open, and I would rather not find out.
